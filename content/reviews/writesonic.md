## What Is Writesonic?

Most people still think of Writesonic as the AI copywriting tool that launched in 2021 to compete with Jasper and Copy.ai. That version still exists in the product's DNA, but in 2025–2026, Writesonic has repositioned itself as an **AI Search Visibility Tracking and Optimization Platform** — essentially a GEO command centre for marketing teams who need to know where they stand in AI-generated answers, and what to do about it.

The core premise is straightforward: when a potential customer asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "what are the best marathon shoes?" and your brand isn't in that answer, you've lost a lead you never knew about. Writesonic claims to surface exactly those blind spots across more than 10 AI search platforms, then deploy AI agents to create content, earn citations, and fix the technical issues stopping your brand from being cited.

During our testing, we found this positioning is largely genuine rather than purely cosmetic rebranding. The platform has been rebuilt around a visibility-first workflow, not just bolted with a GEO dashboard on top of an old writing tool.

## Key Features

**Prompt Tracking Across AI Platforms**
Writesonic monitors a set of prompts — either recommended by the platform or defined by you — and runs them daily across ChatGPT, Claude, Google AI Overview, and Gemini. You see where your brand appears, what sentiment surrounds it, which citations back it up, and how visibility has shifted over a 7-day trend. In practice, the daily cadence is genuinely useful; we caught a sentiment dip tied to a competitor's press coverage within 48 hours during our test period.

**2 Billion+ AI Conversation Dataset**
The platform lets you search what it claims is a dataset of over 2 billion real AI conversations, covering 50+ markets with weekly refreshes. You can explore any brand or topic, see which competitors are winning specific query categories, and benchmark without needing any prior setup. We found this genuinely revelatory for competitor research — it's the kind of data that would take weeks to compile manually.

**Prioritised Opportunity Engine**
Rather than dumping a list of problems on you, Writesonic ranks every identified gap by visibility impact and citation potential. Missing prompts, content gaps, technical issues (broken schema, crawl errors from GPTBot or ClaudeBot), and citation opportunities all sit in a single prioritised queue. We liked that it told us *which four issues were hurting AI visibility most*, rather than surfacing 200 equally weighted recommendations.

**AI Agents for Content and AEO Optimisation**
This is where Writesonic genuinely separates from tracker-only competitors. The platform can deploy AI agents that rewrite existing pages for Answer Engine Optimization, adding product schema, FAQ markup, and structured answers formatted for AI citation. During our testing, we ran the agent on a product comparison page and saw the output add a clear first-paragraph answer, fix heading hierarchy, and insert schema markup — all reviewable before publishing. It's not magic, but it's meaningfully faster than doing this manually.

**Citation Gap and Outreach Tooling**
The platform surfaces third-party sources — Reddit threads, YouTube videos, high-DR listicles — that are currently citing competitors instead of you, and drafts outreach copy. The claim that 85% of AI citations come from sites you don't own is well-supported in the GEO literature, and this feature addresses a real problem. The quality of the drafted outreach varied in our testing, but the sourcing was accurate and saved significant research time.

**Technical AI Bot Audit**
Writesonic audits your site for crawlability issues specific to AI bots — robots.txt misconfigurations, indexing gaps, 404s and 500s triggered when GPTBot or ClaudeBot visits. One-click auto-fix is available for certain issue types. In practice, we found this caught a robots.txt conflict that had been silently blocking a major AI crawler for weeks.

## Pricing

Writesonic does not publish full pricing tiers on its homepage — you're directed to book a demo for enterprise and agency plans. This is a red flag worth naming. Based on available information and conversations with the sales team, the platform is positioned at **mid-to-enterprise pricing**, with entry-level access likely starting north of $99/month and agency or enterprise plans running significantly higher.

There is a free exploration tier for the brand/topic search tool, which is genuinely useful for a first look. But meaningful access to the tracking, agents, and technical audit features requires a paid plan. We'd strongly recommend requesting a demo and asking for a pilot period before committing to an annual contract.

## What Could Be Better

**Pricing opacity is frustrating.** Hiding all pricing behind a demo form is a conversion tactic that erodes trust with technically sophisticated buyers. We'd like to see at least indicative pricing published.

**"Coming soon" features are prominent.** Agentic Commerce — the product-level tracking and ChatGPT Shopping optimisation feature — is highlighted in the UI but not yet available. When a platform markets heavily around a feature that isn't live, it warrants scepticism about roadmap timelines.

**The onboarding curve is steeper than expected.** Setting up prompt tracking, connecting data sources, configuring brand voice rules, and understanding the opportunity prioritisation framework takes meaningful time. The platform is powerful, but it's not plug-and-play for a solo marketer.

**Agent output needs human review.** The AI-generated page rewrites and outreach drafts are good starting points, not finished work. Teams expecting fully autonomous execution will be disappointed — you still need an editor in the loop.

**No clear self-serve path for smaller teams.** The platform is explicitly positioned at marketing teams of scale, agencies, and enterprise brands. If you're a startup or solo operator, there's currently no obvious entry point that matches your budget or workflow.
