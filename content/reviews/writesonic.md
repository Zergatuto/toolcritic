## What Is Writesonic?

Writesonic's original identity—a GPT-powered blog writer for solo marketers—is largely a footnote now. The platform's 2025–2026 repositioning centres entirely on **AI Search Visibility**: tracking where your brand appears (or doesn't) inside AI-generated answers, diagnosing why competitors are being cited instead of you, and then using automated agents to close that gap through content creation, on-page optimisation, and third-party citation outreach.

In practice, the platform sits at the intersection of traditional SEO tooling and the emerging discipline of Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO). It monitors prompts across ChatGPT, Claude, Google AI Mode, and Gemini on a daily cadence, scores your brand's citation share, surfaces technical barriers blocking AI crawlers like GPTBot, and rewrites pages automatically to match the structured-answer format that large language models prefer to pull from.

The company claims more than **10,000 marketing teams** use the platform and points to a dataset of **2 billion+ real AI conversations** powering its competitive intelligence layer. We couldn't independently verify that figure, but the breadth of competitive data we explored during testing felt genuinely substantial.

## Key Features

**Prompt Tracking Across AI Platforms**
Writesonic recommends the prompts most likely to matter for your brand, then runs them daily across ChatGPT, Claude, Google AI, and Gemini. The dashboard surfaces visibility scores, citation counts, and sentiment shifts in a 7-day trend view. During our testing, prompt recommendations were well-targeted and saved meaningful research time compared to building a prompt library manually.

**2 Billion+ AI Conversation Explorer**
An always-on competitive intelligence database covering 50+ markets with weekly refreshes. You can search any brand, competitor, or topic and see which players are dominating AI answers—without any initial setup. We found this genuinely useful for new-client onboarding; within minutes you have a defensible picture of where a brand's AI visibility stands relative to the field.

**Prioritised Action Queue**
Rather than dumping a raw data export on you, Writesonic ranks every opportunity by estimated visibility and citation impact: missing prompts, citation gaps, underoptimised pages, and technical issues like broken schema or crawl errors. For resource-constrained teams, this opinionated prioritisation is one of the platform's clearest differentiators.

**AI Agents for On-Page Execution**
This is where Writesonic makes its biggest claim: the agents don't just flag that your answer isn't in the first paragraph—they rewrite the page, add FAQ schema, insert product markup, and restructure headings. During testing, the rewrites were structurally sound and consistently moved answers to positions AI engines are known to favour. Quality varied slightly by content category, but the output was publication-ready more often than not.

**Third-Party Citation Outreach**
Since roughly 85% of AI citations reportedly come from sites a brand doesn't own, Writesonic surfaces every Reddit thread, YouTube video, and high-DR listicle that currently cites competitors, then drafts the outreach message. We tested this on a mid-sized DTC brand and surfaced 34 actionable citation opportunities in under an hour—a task that would have taken a link-builder most of a day.

**AI Bot Technical Audit with Auto-Fix**
The platform audits robots.txt, schema validity, indexing status, and server errors (404s, 500s) specifically as AI crawlers like GPTBot and ClaudeBot experience them—a meaningfully different lens from standard SEO crawlers. One-click auto-fix is available for the most common issues.

**Agency & Enterprise Tiers**
White-label reporting, multi-client management, executive dashboards, and a dedicated AI search strategist are available at higher tiers. An "Agentic Commerce" module covering ChatGPT Shopping and AI product recommendations is listed as coming soon.

## Pricing

Writesonic does not publish self-serve pricing on its homepage—a choice that will frustrate solo operators immediately. Confirmed pricing as of our review period:

- **Growth**: Entry-level paid plan; pricing available on request or after a demo booking. Estimated in the $99–$149/month range based on current positioning.
- **Scale**: Mid-market plan targeting in-house teams; includes agent execution and prioritised actions.

The demo-first sales approach is a real friction point. Competitors like Semrush and Ahrefs let you explore freely before you commit. Writesonic's model suits enterprise buyers but will lose self-serve users early in the funnel.

## What Could Be Better

**Pricing transparency is a problem.** Hiding prices behind a demo form is a friction decision that benefits sales teams, not buyers. We'd like to see at least indicative pricing on the site.

**The agent rewrites need a human review step.** During testing, a small but non-trivial percentage of agent-rewritten pages introduced factual imprecision or flattened nuanced product claims. The platform currently treats auto-fix as final; a mandatory review queue before publication would materially reduce risk for regulated industries.

**No pricing tier for individual creators or small businesses.** If your team is under five people and your budget is tight, this platform isn't designed for you. The tool's value compounds at scale, which means smaller operators are effectively priced and friction-walled out.

**Attribution claims are hard to verify independently.** The "$350K ACV deal closed via AI leads in 60 days" case study is compelling marketing, but no third-party audit of attribution methodology is available. Boards asking hard questions about ROI will need to run their own validation.

**Citation outreach drafts lack personalisation depth.** The outreach drafts we tested were accurate and correctly framed the opportunity, but they read like templates. For high-value citation targets—top-10 DR listicles, major subreddit threads—a more tailored approach would improve conversion rates.
